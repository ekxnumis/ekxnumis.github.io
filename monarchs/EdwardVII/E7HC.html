Edward VII Halfcrown

The Edward VII halfcrown, valued at two shillings and sixpence, was minted in sterling silver between 1902 and 1910. As one-eighth of a pound, it was a significant denomination in circulation during the Edwardian period. Collectors admire these coins for their impressive size, detailed designs, and the range of subtle obverse and reverse varieties. High-grade examples are particularly prized and can command strong premiums at auction.
