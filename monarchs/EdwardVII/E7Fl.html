Edward VII Florin

The Edward VII florin, valued at two shillings, was struck in sterling silver between 1902 and 1910. It represented one-tenth of a pound and was a key denomination in circulation during the Edwardian era. Collectors today appreciate the series both for its artistry and for the subtle design varieties across obverses and reverses. Well-struck examples are sought after, particularly in higher grades.
