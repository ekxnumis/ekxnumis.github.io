George VI Sixpence

Only three notable changes occurred in this series:

  • Reverse A was replaced by Reverse B in 1943 (a very small adjustment to the border teeth).
  • The final debasement to cupro-nickel took place in 1947, ending silver issues.
  • In 1949, following Indian independence, the royal cypher was changed. The earlier GRI (Georgius Rex Imperator) became G VI R (Georgius VI Rex). Unlike most denominations, which only needed word spacing adjusted, the sixpence required a complete cypher modification.

The only date generally considered a "key" is 1952. While these are very common in VF, they are much harder to find in true mint state.
