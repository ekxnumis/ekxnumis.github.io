Sheldon Scale vs. Descriptive Grading

Compare the American 70-point system to traditional European (UK/CGS) and World descriptive grading standards.

Understanding the Differences

The **Sheldon Scale** (MS-70 to P-1) is a numerical standard primarily used in the U.S. and for certified coins globally. Traditional **Descriptive Grading Systems** (like those often referred to as CGS, or used in the UK/Europe) use terms like Fine, Extremely Fine, and Uncirculated, which cover a much wider range of quality than a single numerical grade.

Note: Conversions are approximate. A coin's appearance can cause its numerical and descriptive grades to shift slightly.

Grade Comparison Chart

Numerical Grade (Sheldon) Descriptive Grade (CGS/UK) Description
**MS-70 to MS-60** **Uncirculated (Unc.) / Fleur de Coin (FDC)** No wear present; as struck. MS-65+ often called "Gem Uncirculated."
**AU-58 to AU-50** **About Uncirculated (AUnc.) / Extremely Fine (EF)** Very slight friction or wear visible on high points; nearly full luster.
**EF-49 to EF-40** **Extremely Fine (EF)** Light wear on the high points. All major details are sharp and clear.
**VF-35 to VF-20** **Very Fine (VF)** Moderate, even wear on the surface. All major design elements are visible.
**F-15 to F-12** **Fine (F)** Considerable wear, but the main features and lettering are clear.
**VG-10 to VG-8** **Very Good (VG)** Heavy wear, but the coin is readable and major features are outlined.
**G-6 to P-1** **Good (G) to Fair (F)** Severely worn. The date and type are identifiable, but most detail is gone.
