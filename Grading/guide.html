Grading Guide

A reference guide to identify the distinguishing features of different coin grades across monarchs.

Elizabeth II

Fine

  • Top edge of dress indistinct

Very Fine

  • Top edge of dress just visible
  • All leaf outlines clear
  • Hairlines beginning to flatten

Extremely Fine

  • Top of dress clear
George VI

Fine

  • Top of ear worn and flat
  • Cheek and hair worn

Very Fine

  • Ear clear
  • Cheek clear but hair still worn

Extremely Fine

  • Hair clear
  • Only slight wear on cheek
George V

Fine

  • Hair detail almost gone
  • Ear worn

Very Fine

  • Ear clear
  • Some hair detail visible

Extremely Fine

  • Hair clear
  • Only slight wear on moustache
Edward VII

Fine

  • Moustache nearly flat
  • Cheek worn

Very Fine

  • Moustache partly clear
  • Cheek clear

Extremely Fine

  • Moustache clear
  • Cheek high points only slightly worn
Victoria – Old Head

Fine

  • Crown worn flat
  • Veil nearly flat

Very Fine

  • Crown partly clear
  • Veil partly clear

Extremely Fine

  • Crown clear
  • Veil clear
Victoria – Young Head

Fine

  • Hair worn flat
  • Laurels flat

Very Fine

  • Hair partly clear
  • Laurels partly clear

Extremely Fine

  • Hair clear
  • Laurels clear
