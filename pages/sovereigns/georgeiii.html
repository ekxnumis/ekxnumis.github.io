George III Sovereign Series

Introduction

The reign of George III (1760–1820) marked a pivotal period in British numismatic history, aligning with Britain’s industrial and imperial expansion. During his rule, the modern gold sovereign was reintroduced in 1817 after the Great Coinage Act of 1816, which established the gold standard. The George III Sovereign featured a classical portrait of the young king and the iconic reverse design of St George slaying the dragon, engraved by Benedetto Pistrucci.

  • This established the standard for future sovereigns in size, design and gold content.
  • The Pistrucci reverse became the hallmark of British sovereigns.

Collectors today prize the early George III sovereigns not only for their historic importance but also their artistry, rarity (especially in certain years), and the pivotal role they played in shaping the gold sovereign’s enduring legacy.

General Information

  • Monarch: George III (1760–1820)
  • Denomination: One Sovereign
  • Date: 1717 - 1720
  • Composition: Gold (0.9167)
  • Weight: Approximately 7.99 grams
  • Diameter: 22.5 mm
  • Thickness: 1.5 mm
  • Orientation: Coin alignment ↑↓
  • Edge: Reeded
  • Obverse: Laureate portrait of King George III facing right with legend around.
  • Obverse Lettering (Latin): GEORGIUS III D:G: BRITANNIAR: REX F:D: 1820
  • Expanded Legend: Georgius III Dei Gratia Britanniarum Rex Fidei Defensor
  • Obverse Lettering Translation: George the Third by the Grace of God King of the Britains Defender of the Faith
  • Reverse: St George slaying the dragon right within Garter with legend around
  • Reverse Lettering (Latin): HONI · SOIT · QUI · MAL · Y · PENSE
  • Reverse Lettering Translation: Shame on he who thinks evil of it
  • Engraver: Benedetto Pistrucci (both obverse and reverse)
  • Mint: Royal Mint (Tower of London)
