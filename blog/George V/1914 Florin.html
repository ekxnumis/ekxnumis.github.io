George V Florin (1911 – 1919)

Identifying the 1914 Florin Reverse Dies C – E • Wyon Design • Numismatic Study

Introduction

The George V Florin of 1914 represents the culmination of the “Pre-War” florin series introduced in 1911. These coins, designed by Sir Bertram MacKennal (obverse) and Edgar Bertram MacKennal with reverse by Leonard Charles Wyon, display fine variations in lettering and rim structure which allow experts to distinguish distinct die pairings.

Three reverse dies are recorded – Reverse C, D and E – each with subtle diagnostic traits affecting the spacing of the legend FLORIN, the alignment of the harp shield, and the treatment of the border beads. This guide outlines those features and provides collectors with a reliable reference to attribute examples correctly.

General Information

  • Monarch: George V (1910 – 1936)
  • Denomination: Florin (2 Shillings)
  • Date: 1914
  • Composition: Sterling Silver (0.925 fine)
  • Weight: 11.31 grams (ASW 0.3364 oz)
  • Diameter: 28.5 mm
  • Edge: Milled
  • Obverse Designer: Sir Bertram MacKennal (BM on truncation)
  • Reverse Designer: Leonard Charles Wyon
  • Legend: GEORGIVS V DEI GRA BRITT OMN REX FID DEF IND IMP / ONE FLORIN TWO SHILLINGS 1914
  • Mint: Royal Mint – London

Reverse Die Diagnostics

Reverse C

  • Top of crown on harp shield points to a border bead.
  • Thin border beads and clear rim definition.
  • Harp crosses sit well away from border beads.
  • FLORIN is set slightly further from the design.
  • Top of the F in FLORIN points to a space between beads.
  • Large lettering throughout.

Reverse D

  • Top of crown on harp shield aligns with a border bead.
  • Fine rim with uniform beads.
  • Harp crosses set away from rim but slightly closer than Reverse C.
  • FLORIN spaced evenly from central design.
  • F points between border beads like Reverse C.
  • Lettering remains large and bold.

Reverse E

  • Top of crown on harp shield points to a border bead (as before).
  • Thin border but slightly more rounded rim.
  • Harp crosses well away from beads with deeper field relief.
  • FLORIN position similar to Reverse D but the F sits slightly misaligned with the L.
  • Lettering size consistent but spacing tighter than C and D.
